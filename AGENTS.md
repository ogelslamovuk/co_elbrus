## Product positioning

Elbrus must not be presented as a generic taxi driver.

Present him as:
- a local driver and personal guide;
- a person born and raised in Upper Fiagdon;
- someone who knows North Ossetia from the inside;
- a calm, reliable local person who can meet, drive, wait, show, advise, and bring guests back;
- someone who can turn a trip into a living, human experience rather than a standard excursion.

Core idea:

> North Ossetia with a person who grew up here.

The site must sell the feeling that the guest now has “their own person in Ossetia”.

Avoid:
- “taxi website” positioning;
- generic tour-agency positioning;
- official guide-with-a-script tone;
- fake luxury;
- fake folk authenticity;
- empty marketing claims.

## Visual concept

# Mountain Road Trip Notebook

The site should feel like a premium modern travel landing page mixed with a road notebook / hand-marked mountain map.

Use:

- hand-drawn route lines;
- marker/crayon/pencil arrows;
- location flags;
- small handwritten-style notes;

Balance:

The hand-drawn style must feel stylish and editorial, not childish.

Do not create a childish crayon site.
Do not create a generic taxi site.
Do not create a generic tourism template.

Act like a demanding creative director and an experienced traveler who has seen too many boring landing pages.

The landing is successful only if:

- the page has a memorable visual identity;
- the page clearly communicates mountains, road, local knowledge, safety, and personality;
- Elbrus feels like the reason to book, not just the person behind the wheel;
